Guli

Product Management & Marketing

Guli (sound is similar to "Stock Calander" in Chinese!) is the project when I worked at Systex as a product & marketing specialist before pursuing my master degree. In Taiwan, it's quite busy for investors from May to August since most companies hold shareholders' meetings, release semi-annual financial reports, and issue dividends. In order to help investors manage their busy schedules, Guli combines with the stock information, calendar, and social media so that users can add events on schedules easily and never miss important meetings by synchronizing events to their phone.

    Takeaway

    Marketing is an important part of UX

    As I explored the UX area later and once I read “Designing for Emotion" in my spare time, I just realized what product manager taught me was providing pleasure experiences to users by “having personality” first. Just like the book mentioned - "having an emotional portrait of the people you are designing for.”

    While I managed the Facebook fan page, I had a chance to communicate with users directly. I kept in mind that my role is a friend - I am passionate about financial markets and willing to share, try to solve users’ problems and answer questions timely, and talk to them just like a friend. UX is not only about interfaces, it’s about how to communicate to your users and provide pleasure experiences as a whole. Use Facebook as a channel for getting feedbacks is useful, and listen to your users and get them involved do help craft better apps!
