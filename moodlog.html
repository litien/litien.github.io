MoodLog

Paper Prototyping

In order to practice paper prototyping and usability testing in class "IMT540: Design Methods for Interactions and Systems", I created a paper prototype for a mobile application that aims to help people better understand how their life is affected by the location they are. It also enables users to input mood and provides behavior analysis.


ROLE

UX Designer & Prototyper

METHODS

Paper Prototype, Usability Testing

DURATION

1 week


Specifications

The prototype will need to be able to support the following activities:

  • Log in and/or register
  • Enter and edit the information about locations
  • Enter information for the four lifestyle metrics (mood, productivity, wellness, creativity, and intellectual stimulation)
  • Track your lifestyle metrics overtime
  • View the relationship between the locations, types of locations and lifestyle metrics

    • Usability Test

    The tasks were required to describe the system to the user and to give them an idea of what task they should do. The usability test task list that I wrote was as follows.


  • Sign Up: You heard about the MoodLog app form friend when you are during the class break and you wanted to try that app on your smart phone. You download the MoodLog app and create a new MoodLog account.

  • Record Mood: You have been doing assignment for several hours in Allen library, and you are not very productive compared with two hours ago. However, since you’ve almost done the assignment, you feel happy about that. Because your mood is different from what you previously recorded when you just entered the library , you want do update your moods and related information on MoodLog app, and you also want to record how long and where you’ve been focused on this assignment.

  • Track Daily Metric: You record the mood and location when you ate breakfast, had four hour class, and went to super market with roommates during the day. Before going to bed, you like to figure out your life style, such as when do you have the best mood, highest productivity and intellectual stimulation.

  • Search: After you use the MoodLog app for couple of days, one day when you have café in HUB, you suddenly want to know what you’ve already recorded at this place. You search the place you are currently lingering, and find out you are always in a good mood and feel creative in this place. After finding the result, you can also search for all the café you’ve recorded if you wonder whether all the café make you feel good. As a result, you can choose another café to change your mood or be inspired next time once you are not near HUB.

    • Result

    Comments from Instructor Alex Jansen (Grade: 4.0)

  • Very good understanding of user task and paper prototyping

    • Takeway

    Paper prototype is an easy way to communicate ideas, catch usability issues and iterate UI quickly, and get user involved at an early stage. It was an fun experience to observe how potential users go through different screens with assigned tasks. After self-reflection and consuming some articles, here are the major elements I think to evaluate using paper prototypes:

  • Navigation structure
  • Concepts and categories
  • Functionality and missing features
  • Task flows, content, and labels
