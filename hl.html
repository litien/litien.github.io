Staff Inbox Redesign

Optimize the process for staff to clean up inbox

HealthLoop helps the care team stay connected with patients through their surgical journey. As hospital staff, they work with multiple doctors and interact with their patients regarding symptoms, comments, and questions.


This goal is to improve staff’s work efficiency by helping them complete their job of responding to patients and shifting responsibility faster. The challenge is to accommodate different size of healthcare organizations since our business is shifting from small and midsize business (SMB) to enterprise solutions.

ROLE

Design Lead

METHODS

Stakeholder Interview, Information Architecture, Task Flow, Wireframe, Prototype, User Testing

CLIENT

HealthLoop

My Role

As the project owner, I’m responsible for the app UI revamp, UX enhancement, and usability testing. I worked with a cross-functional team, including product manager, customer support, and engineers. Contribution included wireframing, task ﬂow, UI revamp, iterations based on internal critique and user studies.

The Users




Throughout the design process, I used the existing staff persona to guide decisions about features, navigation, and interaction. It's also a powerful way to communicate insights about the behavior and pain points of the users.

Problems

Staff Workflow

From initial research with internal stakeholders, grouping lists of tasks in the inbox by patient symptom severity does not meet the goal when staff want to find patients associate with a doctor.


Filter mechanism

Staff apply the filter to view patients by doctor. However, after filtering out doctors, staff forget to switch it back. Therefore, no one takes care of the patients because their information is hidden.


Example of toggle on - view patients of Dr. Haus
Example of toggle off - hide patients of Dr. Haus

Low discoverability of important configurations

As the filter performs the function of displaying and temperately hiding doctors. I went further to exam the interdependency of the app, which is the configuration of handling the doctor list. And I found it's not easy for staff to adjust the doctors they work with because this feature is hidden under the navigation system.


Lack of scalability

Doctor list is not scalable for health systems that have more complex organization hierarchy. Staff users also need a better way to deal with large amount of data.




User Testing

Validate assumptions with users



To deepen the knowledge about staff users' behavior and gather feedback about the new UI changes, I established the research plan, defined tasks, recruited participants, and conducted remote user testing sessions with 6 current HealthLoop staff users to validate the assumptions.



User Feedback

It’s helpful when doctors have different protocols or preferences.

I don’t need to go to the practice setting. It’s a good idea of having the two options of grouping things.

I know physicians I am looking at and covering with.

Solution

Introduce the new sorting feature

Incorporate the use case that providers have different instructions or protocols and help staff increase efficiency to get things done.


Get rid of the filter

Have full control and always see the completed to-do list coming from patients. No more hidden doctor and no more missing information.


Make it easier to access the doctor list

Provide a quick entry point to make the important configuration accessible and easy to find.


Incorporate scalability

Improve browsing and enable scoped search so that staff in a large health system can switch between department and organization level.



Mobile Design

After validating the solution with users and polished the detailed design for the desktop version, I began to work on mobile design and adjusted the features based on the usage frequency and information architecture.
