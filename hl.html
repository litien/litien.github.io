Staff Inbox Redesign

Optimize the process for staff to clean up inbox

Project Background

HealthLoop helps the care team stay connected with patients through their surgical journey. As a first responder (a.k.a medical assistant or nurse practitioner), they work with multiple providers and interact with their patients regarding symptoms, comments, and questions.


This goal of this redesign project is to improve first responder’s work efficiency by optimizing the process of cleaning up inbox to-dos and making the configuration more accessible and intuitive. The challenge is to accommodate both large scale healthcare systems and small-to-mid size practices to meet new business requirements and minimize the changes to reduce the learning curve.

Design Process

Make assumptions and test out the idea with users



  1. Frame the Problem
  2. Brainstorm on Solutions
  3. Prototype for Usability Testing
  4. Validate the Idea with Users
  5. Iterate on Detailed Design

Clean up inbox with flexibility

The new feature of organizing to-dos by providers in addition to symptom severity gives the staff flexibility to clean up inbox based on provider preferences and specialties.


Access to provider list easily

The redesign solves the problem by providing quick entry points and reconstructing the information architecture to make the configuration accessible and easy to find.


Select providers with a clear overview

Whenever adjusting the providers you work with, it provides comprehensive visual indications. This new design also accommodates different health system organizational structures.

UI Changes

To deepen the knowledge about staff’s behavior and ensure that designs get more effective with each cycle of testing, I established the research plan, defined tasks, recruited participants, and conducted remote usability testing sessions with 6 first responders who are currently using our app.



Involve engineers early in the process

As the project lead, I shared the usability plan with engineers and invited them to the usability sessions as it’s critical to have the team understand user frustration and needs through one-on-one sessions where a real user performs tasks.

Mobile Design

Considering the contextual scenario that first responders usually use desktop in the office, I chose Desktop-First approach as the starting point. After validating the solution, I began to work on mobile design and adjusted the features based on the usage frequency and information architecture.
