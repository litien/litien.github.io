Caregiver MVP

Incorporate caregivers to support patients for better outcomes

Challenge

This is the project came from one of the new hospital clients. The challenge was that the date to go-live was very short. The team only had 6 weeks to complete research and design, and it was the first time that we jumped into the caregiver field. Therefore, the whole design team, including one user researcher and three designers were all working on this caregiver MVP project together.

METHODS

Project Goal


Problem Statement

How can we leverage caregivers as a resource to improve patient compliance?




Discovery

The goal was to uncover the spectrum of experiences that family members taking care of a loved one who is sick or injured to get a deep, holistic understanding of the caregiver journey and experience.

User Insights

  • Caregivers as agents are more assertive advocates of the patient’s health than the patients are.
  • Assertive caregivers are seen as nuisance.
  • Caregiver relationships are predictable: one primary & up to several secondaries.
  • Key factor in predicting caregiver hierarchy is proximity.
  • Secondary factors: relationship, personality of possible caregivers.
  • Power dynamic in relationships is dependent on the caregiver’s perception of patient’s assertiveness.
  • Primary caregiving role doesn’t feel like a choice.
