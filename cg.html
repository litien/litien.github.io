Caregiver MVP

Incorporate caregivers to improve better patient outcomes


This is the project came from one of the new hospital clients. The challenge was that the date to go-live was very short. The team only had 6 weeks to complete research and design, and it was the first time that we jumped into the caregiver field. Therefore, the whole design team, including one user researcher and three designers were all working on this caregiver MVP (Minimum viable product) together.

ROLE

Research / Design

METHODS

Interview, UX Flow, Wireframe, Visual Mockups

DURATION

6 weeks

Context

29% of Americans are taking care of loved ones in any given year with an average of 20 hours a week spent on caregiving. Caregivers are a valuable resource that are not being utilized by our application.

Project Goal


Problem Statement

How might we leverage caregivers as a resource to improve patient outcomes by increasing patient compliance?




Discovery

The team first interviewed stakeholders to understand how they think about the problem, business needs, success criteria, and expectations. Then validated hypothesis through user research and customer feedback and identified more problems and opportunities while also increasing the understanding of the motivations and behavior of caregivers.

» User Insights

  • Caregivers as agents are more assertive advocates of the patient’s health than the patients are.
  • Assertive caregivers are seen as nuisance.
  • Caregiver relationships are predictable: one primary & up to several secondaries.
  • Key factor in predicting caregiver hierarchy is proximity.
  • Secondary factors: relationship, personality of possible caregivers.
  • Power dynamic in relationships is dependent on the caregiver’s perception of patient’s assertiveness.
  • Primary caregiving role doesn’t feel like a choice.

    How Does It Work?

    Patients invite loved ones, share information and control different levels of access, and work together to ensure the best outcome.




     Invite Caregivers




     Control Access




     Improve Compliance


    Invite Caregivers

    Patients can easily invite caregivers through email address or phone number.


    Control Access

    The patient can control the caregiver access and invite other caregivers. Therefore, family members get a chance to help coordinate care for a loved one.

    Improve Compliance

    In the activity feed, the check-in status is visible to both the patient and the caregivers. Loved ones can also view the communication thread to know what’s going on and keep updated.
