NoteU

Web App Design & Development

NoteU is an online sticky note that displayed hierarchical information in a flat view and provided checklist by status to let users keep things organized. It's an easy way to let users remember ideas, and stay organized. Through retrieving files from dropbox and implementing Xooml, NoteU makes your life simplier and easier. This is an independent study with a team of 4 people directed by Professor William Jones from University of Washington Information School.



ROLE

UX Design
Front-end Development

METHODS

Competitor Analysis, Persona, Wireframe, Prototype

DURATION

Mar - June 2013




Project Background

Based on keeping found things found, a Personal Information Management (PIM) project, we built end-user web application that uses itemMirror objects with drivers that target information contained within Dropbox.

Applications built by different teams each work with the same folder hierarchies as shared through Dropbox but provide different functionality, such as note-taking, check-list management, outlining and mind-mapping.

Design Process

  • Gather requirements from the stakeholder and understanded the project goal and technical requirements.
  • Brainstorm the app concept and features based on the topic and requirements.
  • Research on similar web apps and build personas and scenarios.
  • Wireframe all elements and ensure smooth communication with the stakeholder and engineers.
  • Execute the design with engineers, take charge the front-end development, and market it to the judges.

    • Challenge

    Technical Requirements

    Make users easy to retrieve and store information become effortless, and follow the existing Xooml Schema - for example, if there is no function of "color", then we can't have multiple colors for our sticky note, same as date and time.


    Features

  • Keep things on track by checklist
  • Intuitive drag & drop features to organize lists
  • Assign sub-tasks within lists
  • Integrate the concept of files into checklist
  • Share checklists and files with others through Dropbox
  • Store things without database
