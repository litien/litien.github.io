Seattle Crime Map

Data Visualization

This project was produced for INFX 598 Data Visualization instructed by Professor Jaime Snyder at the University of Washington. It's an interactive data visualization of 2014 Seattle crime data. The goal is to help business owners and the general public identify major crime types and numbers among their living areas and then make further decisions.


ROLE

Design / Development

METHODS

Sketches, User Testing, Implemented with D3.js

DURATION

March - June 2015


Design Process

  • Analyze a dataset from "Seattle Police Department Police Report Incident" and clean unnecessary data.
  • Define the target audience and requirements.
  • Identify the motivation and questions to which the visualization will respond.
  • Brainstorm with different visual encoding and graphic design choices through a series of sketches.
  • Conduct 5 user testings to find out pros and cons that the visualization can improve on.
  • Iterate and modify visualization in response to evaluation findings.
  • Implement the final data visualization with D3.js.

    • The Audience

    Business owners usually care about the safety information around their business district, so people who run business in Seattle area are our audience. Business owners are assumed to have some knowledge about the Seattle area. Knowledge of crime types and the ability of reading maps is required for this group.

  • New business owners: plan to open a local store can use this visualization to choose a place.
  • Current business owners: know the crime situation of their living district and make further related decisions for the business.

    • The general public could include families or students who care about their safety in Seattle. People are assumed to have little or basic knowledge about the Seattle area and criminal types.

  • People who are trying to find a new place to move in can use the visualization as a reference to select the new living district.
  • People who care about the crime situation in their living district can use the visualization to understand the safety situation.
  • The audience can gain crime occurrence information based on locations and compare among crime types.

    • Ideation

    From previous user studies evaluations, we realized that the users preferred the map view in the visualization. They also mentioned they expected to see an interactive map to learn more detailed information about different districts. Based on these feedbacks, we decided to use the Seattle map as a basic component of the visualization. Viewers can explore the visualizations by mousing over an area in the map in each sketch.


    User Testing

    participant selection

    5 participants were involved in our usability study. They are all selected from our targeted user groups:




    Detail of the protocol



    Step1: Introduction

    Introduced the project background and the team, the purpose of the usability testing and methods applied, and the following process to selected participants.


    Step2: User Background

    Understand users’ basic information, experience of using visualization to make a decision, and any information they are curious about when thinking of the crime visualization.


    Step3: Testing

    Let participants apply think-aloud method in order to gather general feedbacks, purpose of using, pros & cons of each sketch, and its effectiveness.


    Iteration

    4 of 5 participants said the sketch #2 is the most useful visualization for them to explore the crime situation and make further decisions. According to the feedbacks, here is the list of improvements:

  • Indication: Low opacity background is added on Parallel Coordinates chart for each district so that it would give the audience a basic idea about the general crime situation in Seattle without clicking anything.
  • Interaction: When clicking a district on the map, the area corresponding to the selected area will be highlighted.
  • Labeling: For the selected area, the district name and an overall crime occurrence number are added to indicate the area’s detailed information.
  • Visual Style: When we showed the concept design with the basic styling, some users felt the black background with the red color chart is a little bit scary, though the initial idea is that darker color scheme would be appropriate for the topic. Hence, white background is applied in the final visualization.
