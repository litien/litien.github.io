Carmax

Windows Phone App Design

This group project was completed for COM589: Interactive Design Course instructed by Jason Levine at University of Washington. The problem is that customers of CarMax are commonly undecided on the exact car they wish to purchase. Knowing what car type, make, model and options to select as the criteria for a buyer can be a confusing and a long and drawn out process. This often leads to potential customers leaving the dealership. Therefore, the goal is to design a mobile application that simplifies the car selection process and increases customers' shopping intention.



ROLE

UX Design / Prototype

METHODS

Competitor Analysis, Interview, Persona, Sketches, Sitemap, User Flow, Wireframe, Prototype

DURATION

March - June 2013


Goal


Persona


Research: User Interview

User interview is the first step of the project. The goal is to understand users based on the given persona, and figure out how they feel about car dealers and pain points. The participants have either purchased a car in the past three months or are looking to buy a car recently. Due to time constraints and resource availability, the users we interviewd are first 2 personas - decided by lifestyle and functionality.4 face-to-face interviews are conducted at the stage.


List of findings:
  • The distrust feeling toward dealers
  • Would love to get recommendations from friends
  • Hard to calculate the ideal car meeting budget
  • Complexity of filtering searching results
  • Have difficulty managing the list of cars they were interested in

    • Ideation

    According to the interview findings and the product research, we brainstormed with lots of sketches. The idea is to apply the concept of gamification to lower customers' distrust feeling toward dealers and also make the process more entertaining.
