Transcend

iOS App Design

Do you ever feel like you'd be lost without your phone, or find it hard to put it down? Are you curious to know what it’s like to be a little less dependent on your phone and a little more in charge of how and when you use it? Transcend provides a fun, simple way to help college students take control of their technology use and explore new life experiences.

Overview

The Transcend app is my capstone project for master degree at UW. In this project, our sponsor is interested in whether there’s an app that could encourage app-enablement while discouraging app-dependence based on the concept proposed in the book “The App Generation."

Project Duration

The project has 2 phases. Phase 1 took place between January and March 2015. It was about project planning and user research. Phase 2 was to implement the design and ship the project. It happened between April and June, and included 2 round of iterations.

My Role

I led the team of 3 people, including 1 designer and 2 developers and was responsible for the experience design of the iOS app. I lead the UX work, producing all major deliverables and presenting the design concept, and negotiating and confirming features with the sponsor.


Project Background


In The App Generation (2013, Yale University Press), Howard Gardner and our sponsor Katie Davis, Assistant Professor from UW Information School, argue that digital media technologies open up exciting new opportunities for young people to explore their identities, connect with other people and ideas, and express themselves creatively. At the same time, the research suggests that there can sometimes be too much of a good thing.


In the book, the authors make the distinction between app-enablement and app-dependence. App-enablement is what we hope to see in all youth: using technology as a gateway to new experiences, relationships, and ideas. App-dependence is what we'd like to avoid: approaching technology uncritically and expecting it to supply the answers to all of life's questions and decisions.


Design Process

  • Understand the project background and gather requirements from the stakeholder.
  • Plan for the design and development timeline.
  • Research on users by conducting interview and analyze competitors in the market.
  • Create 3 personas based on interview findings.
  • Generate design concepts, ideate on options, and prioritize features.
  • Execute UI flow, visual design and deliver the interactive prototype.

    Persona


    Moodboard



    Introducing Transcend

    You earn and achieve your badges while recording focused time engaged in a range of self-selected activities, challenges, and reflections. Experience a sense of accomplishment by charting your activities and cultivating time management skills. Aim control of your technology use by monitoring your usage hours everyday. Gain personal insight by reflecting on your technology habits. Transcend provides a self-directed method for helping you to shift the balance in your life from app-dependence to app-enablement.



    3 steps to motivate staying focused and shift behaviors





     1. Become Self Conscious

     Chart activities and start to play a game that helps stay focused and be aware of technology use behaviors.




    2. Gain Personal Insights

     Understand the personal habit of using technology through data collection and activity progress tracking.




     3. Build Confidence

     Cultivate time management pattern by earning badges and experience a sense of accomplishments.

    Takeaway

    Creativity vs. Feasibility

    In this project, what I learned is not only thinking from users’ perspectives, but also seriously considering “risks” that could happen during the implementation stage. Even if you have confirmed with developers and make sure the team finds a balance between creativity and feasibility, there are still risks of change due to developer’s capability and time constraints. Sometimes, we aren’t lack of ideas, what we need is people who can execute and implement the idea. Designers should start from the point of keeping the design user-centered, however, we also need to communicate the feasibility with developers, and understand how many resources we have in order to find the best approach to make things happen!
